QR Coffee Rewards Prototype

Evolus Pencil Camtasia Studio User Experience (UX)

When: January-March 2015

Contribution: Usability Interviews, Low-Fi / High-Fi Prototype Design, Video Production, Target Population Identification, Meeting Facilitation

Description: QR Rewards is a prototype of a customer loyalty app that awards and redeems points for purchases at small coffee shops. This project was created by a team of students in Josh Tenenberg's Human Computer Interaction/Design course (TCSS 452) at the University of Washington in Tacoma.

This project focused on first identifying specific pain points experienced by a target population and then designing a technology-based solution to address the pain. Our team utilized contextual inquiries, hi-fi & low-fi prototypes, storyboards, usability interviews and class presentations to design and refine our solution across multiple iterations. The video for this project identifies our target population, highlights specific pain points and offers our team's solution.

U We Tech Game

Javascript HTML5 Github

When: January-March 2015

Contribution: Game Engine, Version Control Consistency, Fowler's Puzzle, Game Design, General Debugging and Code Cleanup

Description: U We Tech is a 2D game built using Javascript and HTML5 canvas. This project was created by a team of students in Chris Marriott's Computational Worlds (TCSS 491) at the University of Washington in Tacoma.

The game's map is a facsimile of the University of Washington in Tacoma and features cameos of students, teachers and staff from the campus.
